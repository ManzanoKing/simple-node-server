The World War II Era

The Internet started over 50 years ago, with computers serving their main military-based purpose -- communication. Until the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1 to orbit around the globe for scientific and military purposes, this signaled the beginning of a space race between the U.S. and U.S.S.R. Thus the U.S. established the Advanced Research Program Agancy (ARPA) a year later; for research development and application, and the development of technologically-advanced and development-based civilian and military projects. And in 1960, ARPA surrendered its civilian space programs to the National Aeronautics and Space Administration(NASA) and its military programs to the U.S. Department of Defense. Leaving ARPA focused on computer science and information processing. Thus, the birth of ARPANET and Transmission Control Protocol/ Internet Protocol (TCP/IP) on the successing years.
