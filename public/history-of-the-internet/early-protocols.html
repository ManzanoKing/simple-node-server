The Early Protocols: ARPANET, TCP/IP

In 1962, a scientist named J.C.R.Licklider proposed the idea of a network of computers that can talk with one another. And in 1969, the ARPANET, U.S. government's computer network, was used and successfully transmited a message between two computers. In 1971, Vinton Cerf introduced a way for two computers across the globe to connect via a virtual space, thus the Transmission Control protocol, evetually Internet Protocol. TCP/IP, a standard which guaranteed compatibility between networks, when combined serves as the language governing communications among all computers on the Internet.
